Fractional CFO
Series A SaaS · $9M Revenue · Pre-Raise

Finance function rebuilt four months before a fundraise.

Situation
Finance manager had just left. Board packs built the night before meetings. Fundraise four months away. Investors asking questions that took a week to answer.
Complication
Rebuilding the model revealed two revenue streams reported gross when they should be net — ARR overstated by ~$1.1M. Needed correction before investor conversations began.

ARR corrected by $1.1M. KPI framework, rolling forecast, and board cadence built from scratch. Round closed on schedule — no diligence adjustments.

Fundraising Readiness
Pre-Series A B2B SaaS · $4M ARR

A strong product with a model that didn't tell the story.

What we found
CAC wasn't fully allocated. Payback period extended from 11 to 17 months when corrected — still fundable, but a very different investor conversation.

3-statement model rebuilt driver-based. Series A closed 11 weeks after investor materials were completed.

Financial Due Diligence
Family Office · $14M Revenue · eCommerce Acquisition

Revenue that looked clean until it was stress-tested.

What the diligence found
41% revenue concentrated in one wholesale customer on a contract expiring in 8 months. Working capital peg discrepancy adjusted the price by $380k. Neither visible in summary financials.

Buy-side FDD delivered. Deal closed at revised terms — concentration risk renegotiated into earn-out structure.

Financial Due Diligence

Buy-side and sell-side Quality of Earnings for $5M–$100M transactions. Big 4 rigour delivered by the senior person you spoke to.

Most financial due diligence reports tell you what happened. A rigorous QoE tells you what the numbers actually mean — and what risks are embedded in the deal you're about to sign.

What the engagement covers
Quality of EarningsNormalised EBITDA, non-recurring adjustments, and revenue quality — so you know what you're actually buying.
Revenue AnalysisComposition by channel, customer, cohort, and contract — so concentration risk and churn are visible before close.
Working Capital ReviewPeg calculation, normalisation, and working capital bridge — so the price reflects the actual business, not a summary P&L.
Net Debt ReviewDebt-like items, contingent liabilities, and cash-like adjustments affecting the final purchase price.
Risk IdentificationRed flags, deal risks, and R&W considerations — so you enter negotiation with open eyes.
